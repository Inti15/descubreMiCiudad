Palace of Fine Arts

The Palace of Fine Arts is one of the most representative buildings of
Mexico City as its architecture recalls the Baroque era.
was built in the mandate of Porfirio Diaz at the beginning of the century of the
Enlightenment

Monument to the Revolution

The monument to the revolution was built to commemorate the Mexican
Revolution carried out in 1910 is of a beauty architectural singular
having similarities with the Arc de Triomphe located in France

Inbursa Aquarium

The Inbursa Aquarium is a family entertainment place located in Polanco in Mexico City, where you can appreciate the marine species and know information about them, so, besides being fun you learn.

The Chapultepec Fair

The Chapultepec Fair is one of the most visited places for children in Mexico, as well as having a amusement park and mechanical games in the surroundings, there is also a zoo and an artificial lake with canoes to traverse it.

House of the puppets

The cultural Center: puppetry, House of Marionettes is a space where the arts will be integrated with the puppet to develop artistic, didactic and social programs for children, young people. It is a meeting place for families where they can see and explore the Performing arts (theatre, puppets, dance, cinema), exhibitions (plastic arts, photography, cinema), projections (cinema, animations and puppets), in various programs and projects with an annual and constant programming.

Universum Museum

Universum, the Museum of Sciences of the National Autonomous University of Mexico is a pioneer-in its kind-in Latin America. In an environment of more 10 hectares of ecological reserve zone of the Pedregal of San Ángel and Jardinadas areas, Universum has 25.000 m2 constructed of which 12.000 m2 are destined for permanent exhibitions.
